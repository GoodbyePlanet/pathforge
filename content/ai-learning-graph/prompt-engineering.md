# Prompt Engineering

Prompt engineering is the practice of designing inputs to language models to reliably elicit desired outputs.
It bridges the gap between a model's raw capabilities and specific task requirements.

## Why It Matters

Language models are sensitive to how questions are framed. The same underlying capability can produce dramatically
different results depending on prompt structure, examples, and instructions.

## Core Techniques

- **[[few-shot-learning]]** — providing examples in the prompt to guide behavior
- **[[chain-of-thought]]** — asking the model to reason step-by-step before answering
- **[[prompt-engineering-guides]]** — curated resources and guides for mastering prompt design
- **[[context-engineering]]** — designing what information the model sees and when
