# Natural Language Processing

Natural Language Processing (NLP) is the branch of AI concerned with enabling computers to understand, generate, and reason about human language.

## Core Problems

- **Text classification** — spam detection, sentiment analysis, topic labeling
- **Generation** — completing, summarizing, or translating text
- **Understanding** — extracting meaning, answering questions, resolving references

## Key Topics

- **[[tokenization]]** — how text is broken into units that models can process
- **[[embeddings]]** — representing words and sentences as vectors in semantic space

## The Transformer Era

Prior to transformers, NLP relied on rule-based systems and recurrent networks. The introduction of the Transformer architecture in 2017 ("Attention Is All You Need") fundamentally changed the field. Modern large language models (GPT, Claude, Gemini) are all Transformer-based.

## Practical NLP Today

Most NLP tasks are now addressed by prompting pre-trained language models rather than training task-specific models from scratch. Understanding tokenization and embeddings helps you work more effectively with these systems.
